Petfolio: Cross-Platform Pet Care App

Flutter · Dart · Firebase

Overview

Petfolio is a cross-platform Flutter application that helps households coordinate pet care. It provides a shared source of truth for routines, responsibilities, and handoffs so that feeding, medication, and other care tasks are clear, even when multiple people are involved.

Problem

In many homes, pet care is shared across family members, roommates, or pet sitters. Information about schedules and responsibilities is often scattered across text threads, sticky notes, or memory, which makes it easy to miss doses, double-feed, or lose context when someone new steps in to help.

Approach

I designed Petfolio as a shared, structured view of each pet’s care plan. Flutter provides a single codebase for iOS and Android, while Firebase powers authentication, real-time data, and notifications. I used Riverpod for state management and a repository pattern to keep UI, business logic, and persistence concerns clearly separated.

Tech stack

  • Flutter and Dart for a cross-platform mobile UI.
  • Firebase Authentication, Firestore, and Storage for user, pet, and media data.
  • Riverpod and a repository pattern for predictable, testable state management.
  • Local, timezone-aware notifications for feeding and medication reminders.

Key decisions & tradeoffs

  • Chose Firebase to prioritize iteration speed and built-in offline support over full control of backend infrastructure.
  • Modeled users, pets, and care plans explicitly in Firestore to keep access control and sharing rules understandable at a glance.
  • Focused v1 on core routines and handoffs, intentionally deferring advanced analytics and complex reporting to future iterations.

What I learned

This project sharpened my ability to design data models that balance day-to-day usability with long-term maintainability. It also forced me to think carefully about offline behavior, notifications, and role-based access in a consumer-facing context.
